About Plant Groups and Subgroups

The boxes above show how we have grouped over 1,200 plants in Concord, Massachusetts, into six major categories, which we call groups. These groups contain smaller subsets of species we have organized into subgroups. These are not scientific categories, but rather groupings of superficially similar plants that seem to belong together. The goal is to make identification easier.
